Warm Kittens

Keeping kittens warm is vital for their health, especially in the first few weeks when they cannot regulate their body temperature. Use heating pads specifically designed for pets, set to a safe, low temperature, and place them under a blanket or towel to prevent burns. Alternatively, hot water bottles wrapped in soft cloth can be used but need regular refilling.

Create a cozy and insulated space using a sturdy box or pet carrier lined with soft, clean bedding like blankets or towels. Keep the bedding dry and change it frequently. Position the box in a draft-free area away from windows and doors. The ideal temperature for newborn kittens is between 85-90°F (29-32°C) for the first week, gradually decreasing to around 75°F (24°C) by the fourth week.

Monitor the kittens' body temperature regularly. Signs of being too cold include lethargy, lack of appetite, and crying. If a kitten is too cold, warm them gently with your body heat or warm towels, avoiding rapid temperature changes. Consult a veterinarian if you have concerns about a kitten’s temperature or health.
